We Built an AI Tutor for the 2026 PMP Exam — Powered by Claude

March 29, 2026 8 min read AI Coach, Product Update

What if you could ask a PMP expert any question, at any time, and get a detailed answer in seconds? That’s exactly what we built.

Today we’re announcing the AI Coach — a new feature in PM Mastery that gives you instant, personalized PMP exam tutoring powered by Claude, one of the most advanced AI models available. It’s not a generic chatbot. It’s a PMP specialist that understands the 2026 exam format, the new ECO, PMBOK 8th Edition, and every formula you’ll need.

Why We Built This

Studying for the PMP is hard. You answer a practice question, get it wrong, read the explanation, and still don’t fully understand why. You want to ask “but why isn’t option C correct?” or “can you explain this a different way?” But static explanations can’t answer follow-up questions.

The AI Coach can.

What Makes It Different From ChatGPT

You could paste a PMP question into ChatGPT and get a decent answer. But the AI Coach is purpose-built for the PMP exam in ways a general chatbot can’t match:

  • It knows the 2026 ECO. The new exam weights People at 33%, Process at 41%, and Business Environment at 26%. The AI Coach references these domains in every explanation.
  • It thinks like PMI. The exam doesn’t test what you’d do in the real world — it tests what PMI wants you to do. The AI Coach explains the “PMI mindset” behind every answer.
  • It reads your quiz questions. When you get a question wrong in PM Mastery, click “Ask AI Coach” and it already has the full question, all four options, which one you picked, and the correct answer. No copying and pasting.
  • It knows your weak areas. The AI Coach reads your analytics — your accuracy by domain, your difficulty breakdown, your overall progress — and tailors its explanations to what you need most.
  • It covers all the formulas. EVM (PV, EV, AC, CV, SV, CPI, SPI, EAC, ETC, VAC, TCPI), PERT, Communication Channels, EMV, PTA — ask it to explain any formula with real numbers.

How It Works

The AI Coach is available in two ways:

From any quiz question: Answer a question, see the feedback, and click the gold “Ask AI Coach” button. The coach loads the full question context and gives you a personalized breakdown — why the right answer is right, why each wrong answer is tempting, and what PMI principle is being tested.

As a standalone page: Go to the AI Coach anytime from the Study menu. Ask it anything — “Explain the ADKAR model,” “What’s the difference between Scrum and Kanban on the PMP?” “Give me 3 exam tips for the Business Environment domain.” It has quick-prompt buttons to get you started.

Smart Model Routing

Behind the scenes, the AI Coach uses two different Claude models to optimize quality and speed. Simple definition questions (“What is a WBS?”) route to a faster, lighter model for instant responses. Complex reasoning questions (“Walk me through why B is correct using the PMI mindset”) route to a more powerful model for deeper analysis. You don’t need to think about this — it happens automatically.

Personalized Coaching

This is where it gets powerful. The AI Coach doesn’t just answer questions — it adapts to you.

If you’ve answered 200 questions and your Business Environment accuracy is 54%, the AI Coach knows that. When you ask about a concept in that domain, it gives you extra context, simpler analogies, and targeted study tips. If you’re scoring 85% in People domain, it challenges you with edge cases and exam traps instead of basics.

This level of personalization isn’t available on any other PMP prep platform.

What You Can Ask

The AI Coach handles anything PMP-related:

  • Concept explanations: “Explain servant leadership for the PMP exam”
  • Formula breakdowns: “Walk me through EAC with a real example”
  • Question analysis: “Why is this the correct answer? Break it down for me”
  • Comparisons: “Scrum vs Kanban — what does the PMP test?”
  • Exam strategy: “Give me 3 tips for time management on exam day”
  • Framework deep-dives: “Explain the ADKAR change model step by step”
  • Follow-up questions: “But what about in an agile context?”
